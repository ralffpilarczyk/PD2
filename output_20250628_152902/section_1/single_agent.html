Section 1: Operating Footprint

Fu Yu Corporation Limited (the "Group" or "Fu Yu") is a Singapore-headquartered manufacturer of high-end precision plastic and metal components and products, operating through a vertically-integrated business model. The Group's operational footprint spans strategically located manufacturing sites across Asia, supporting a diverse international customer base.

a. Employee distribution across locations and functions

The Group's workforce primarily consists of permanent, full-time employees, complemented by temporary staff to meet operational needs. Its employee base has seen a slight reduction in recent periods, while maintaining an equal gender distribution in FY2024.

d. Any significant changes or trends in the operating footprint

  • Cessation of Supply Chain Management Services Segment: In the last quarter of FY2024, the Group ceased all business activities in Fu Yu Supply Chain Solutions Pte Ltd (FYSCS) to reallocate resources and sharpen focus on its core manufacturing business. This resulted in the full impairment of goodwill related to this segment. 41
  • Increased Focus on Manufacturing: The Group's core manufacturing segment showed a sharp improvement in FY2024, driven by higher order volumes and new projects in biomedical and consumer segments, indicating a successful strategic transformation. 42
  • Investment in Advanced Manufacturing (Smart Factory): The Singapore Smart Factory is central to the Group's advanced precision manufacturing capabilities. Completion of the redevelopment project at 9 Tuas Drive 1 is implied by the Nil cost incurred as of 31 Dec 2024 compared to S$21,046k in 2023. 43
  • Automation and Digitalization: The Group completed most of the testing for its Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) integrated cloud-based IT platform in FY2024, aiming to increase toolroom automation levels by 40% to reduce downtime. There is also an active transition from hydraulic machinery to energy-efficient electric alternatives to enhance operational efficiency. 44
  • Renewable Energy Integration: Installation of solar panels was completed in Singapore facilities in FY2024, and in Fu Hao, Malaysia, by Q4 FY2024. Panels in Classic Advantage, Malaysia, are expected to be operational by Q2 FY2025. This supports a greener footprint and reduces reliance on conventional energy. 45
  • Order Book Growth: The Group's order book increased 20% year-on-year at the end of FY2024, reflecting higher order volumes and interest from existing customers and new project wins. 46
  • Reduction in Waste: Hazardous waste decreased by 25.7% in FY2024 due to fewer large tonnage machine oil changes. Non-hazardous waste also declined, attributed to improved production efficiency and waste reduction initiatives. 47

e. How the operating structure supports competitive positioning

Fu Yu's operating structure is designed to leverage its long-standing expertise and advanced capabilities to maintain a competitive edge:

  • Vertically-Integrated Services: Offering a one-stop solution from product and tooling design, fabrication, production (plastic and metal injection moulding), to assembly and testing enhances value-add for customers and fosters long-term partnerships. 48
  • Technological Leadership: The flagship Smart Factory in Singapore, with its 100K-class cleanroom, high-precision tooling, and 3D metal printing capabilities, positions Fu Yu as one of Asia's most advanced precision manufacturing facilities. This supports the production of complex, high-margin products in sectors like life sciences and medical. 49
  • New Product Introduction (NPI) Team: The NPI team adds value in the early stages of the business cycle, building customer confidence for scaling production while maintaining quality. This has led to securing new projects in medical devices, drone technology, and smart appliances. 50
  • Geographical Flexibility: With manufacturing sites in Singapore, Malaysia, and China, the Group offers geographical flexibility, a critical advantage for customers seeking to diversify production away from China amidst geopolitical uncertainties. 51
  • Operational Excellence and Efficiency: Investments in Industry 4.0 machinery, the ERP system for increased automation, and the transition to electric machinery reduce downtime and improve efficiency. The focus on stabilizing 5-micron precision levels and moving to higher precision enhances product quality and competitiveness. 52
  • Quality and Environmental Certifications: Extensive ISO and IATF certifications (ISO 9001, ISO 13485 for medical, ISO 14001 for environmental, IATF 16949 for automotive) demonstrate a commitment to quality and compliance across diverse industries, reinforcing customer trust and access to regulated markets. 53
