## SECTION 21: Sellside Positioning - Competitive Positioning

Fu Yu Corporation Limited ("Fu Yu" or the "Company"), Singapore's oldest and one of Asia's largest manufacturers of high-end precision plastic and metal components, strategically pivoted to focus solely on its core manufacturing business in FY2024, ceasing all supply chain management services. While the manufacturing segment demonstrated internal improvements, the Group as a whole remained in a net loss (S\$3.9 million in FY2024, narrowed from S\$10.1 million in FY2023).

Critically, the ongoing investigation into the discontinued Fu Yu Supply Chain Solutions Pte Ltd ("FYSCS") has resulted in a qualified audit opinion for FY2024 (AR 24, p.104), signaling material weaknesses in internal controls and potentially unreliable financial statements. While management asserts this does not impact continuing operations (AR 24, p.6), this is an overly simplistic claim. The investigation implies deeper issues, including potential significant financial liabilities beyond the S\$1.67 million arbitration ruling already against FYSCS (AR 24, p.171), and a US\$2.98 million pre-paid commission claim (AR 24, p.100). These non-operational risks directly impact investor perception, the cost of capital, and financial flexibility, potentially undermining operational gains and diverting crucial management attention. The Group's healthy net cash position of S\$55.5 million (AR 24, p.8) becomes less comforting when faced with indeterminate liabilities.
