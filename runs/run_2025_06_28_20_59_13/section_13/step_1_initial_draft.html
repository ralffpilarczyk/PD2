SECTION 13: Strategic Objectives and Corporate Strategies

This section provides a comprehensive analysis of Fu Yu Corporation Limited's strategic objectives (future goals) and corporate strategies (current competitive approaches leveraging existing assets and capabilities), drawing insights from its FY2024 Annual Report, Q4 2024 Unaudited Condensed Interim Financial Statements, and 1H2024 Results Briefing presentation.

a. Strategic Objectives:

Strategic objectives are defined as the company's goals going forward, representing its aspirations and what it aims to achieve in the future. Fu Yu's top 3 strategic objectives for the period reflect its commitment to growth, technological leadership, and sustainability.

  1. Objective 1: Achieve Sustainable Revenue Growth and Improve Financial Performance

    Description: Fu Yu aims to realize increased revenue and improve its financial profitability, building on the strategic transformation initiated in FY2023. This involves securing higher order volumes from existing customers and engaging new clients, particularly in higher-margin product categories.1

    • Timeframe/Horizon:
      • Short-term: Business momentum expected to grow in 2H2024 and financial performance for FY2025 is expected to improve compared to FY2024.2
      • Medium-term: Long-term sustainable revenue from high-precision projects ready for mass production.3
    • Success Metrics/KPIs:
      • Increase in manufacturing revenue: 10.5% growth in FY2024 to S$114.9 million (from S$103.9 million in FY2023).4
      • Order book growth: 20% year-on-year increase by end of FY2024.5
      • Gross profit margin improvement: Manufacturing segment GPM improved to 13.5% in FY2024 (from 11.7% in FY2023).6
      • Net loss narrowing: Net loss for continuing operations narrowed sharply to S$1.2 million in FY2024 (from S$7.6 million in FY2023).7
      • EBITDA from continuing operations: Increased more than 14-fold to S$8.5 million in FY2024 (from S$0.6 million in FY2023).8
    • Resource Allocation:
      • Reallocation of resources from the supply chain arm to the core manufacturing business (ceased FYSCS operations in Q4 FY2024).9

  2. Objective 2: Advance Technological Capabilities and Move Up the Value Chain in Precision Engineering

    Description: The company is focused on enhancing its technological prowess, particularly within its Smart Factory, to produce increasingly complex and high-precision products. This involves increasing automation and improving precision levels.10

    • Timeframe/Horizon:
      • Short-term: Increase automation levels in the toolroom for overnight and weekend production (from FY2024).11
      • Near-term: Stabilizing high-precision products at the 5-micron precision level and moving to even higher levels (from FY2024).12
    • Success Metrics/KPIs:
      • Toolroom downtime reduction: Target to reduce by 40% compared to a typical facility.13
      • Precision level: Achieving 5-micron precision level and advancing further.14
      • Successful mass production of gestating high-precision projects.15
    • Resource Allocation:
      • Capital expenditure: S$6.9 million in FY2024, mainly for purchase of factory equipment, plant and machineries.16
      • Continued investment in Industry 4.0 machinery and Smart Factory optimization.17

  3. Objective 3: Enhance Environmental Stewardship and Sustainability Disclosures

    Description: Fu Yu is committed to integrating sustainable business practices into its core strategy, aiming to reduce its environmental footprint and increase transparency in its sustainability efforts.18

    • Timeframe/Horizon:
      • Short-term: Reduce Scope 2 carbon emissions by 3.0% in FY2024 (from FY2023 baseline).19 (Achieved actual reduction of 1.4% for FY2024).20
      • Medium-term: Reduce Scope 2 overall emissions by 30% by FY2030 (from FY2023 baseline).21
      • Long-term: Strengthening sustainability disclosures and exploring cleaner energy solutions.22
      • Ongoing: Progressively replacing hydraulic machinery with electric alternatives and incorporating 3D printing.23
    • Success Metrics/KPIs:
      • Electricity consumption: 2.5% decrease to 113,623 GJ in FY2024 (from 116,481.6 GJ in FY2023).24
      • Water consumption: 9.1% decrease to 89.1 Megaliter in FY2024 (from 98.0 Megaliter in FY2023).25
      • Hazardous waste reduction: 25.7% reduction in FY2024 (from 76.0 tonnes in FY2023 to 56.4 tonnes).26
      • Solar panel installations: Completed in Singapore facilities in FY2024, operational in Fu Hao (Johor) in Q4 FY2024, and Classic Advantage (Penang) by Q2 FY2025.27
    • Resource Allocation:
      • Investment in energy-efficient electric machines and LED lighting (FY2024).28
      • Investment in solar panel installations across facilities (Singapore, Malaysia).29
      • Allocation of Sustainability Steering Committee (SSC) and Sustainability Task Force (STF) for sustainability initiatives.30

b. Corporate Strategies:

Corporate strategies are defined as how the company is currently competing, utilizing its existing assets and capabilities to achieve its strategic objectives. These strategies are feasible and are actively being implemented.

  1. Strategy 1: Focus on High-Precision Manufacturing and Vertically-Integrated, One-Stop Solutions

    Description: Fu Yu leverages its over 45 years of operating knowledge and advanced capabilities to provide high-precision plastic and metal components, along with comprehensive, vertically-integrated services. This strategy positions the company as a trusted manufacturing partner for innovative, high-performance products across diverse sectors.31

    • Required Capabilities:
      • Expertise in high-end precision plastic and metal component manufacturing.32
      • Advanced tooling capabilities, including complex mould inserts and high-precision 3D metal printing for rapid prototyping.33
      • New Product Introduction (NPI) team for early-stage value addition in the business cycle.34
      • Vertically-integrated services covering product design, tooling design and fabrication, production, assembly, and testing.35
      • 100K-class cleanroom for biomedical product manufacturing and assembly.36
    • Competitive and Historical Context:
      • "Singapore's oldest and one of Asia's largest manufacturers of high-end precision plastic and metal components and products," established in 1978.37
      • FY2024 performance is "a testament to the early successes of our strategy to focus on what we do best â€“ high-precision manufacturing."38
      • Secured new projects in medical devices, drone technology, and smart appliances in FY2024.39
      • New customer base includes global leaders in wound care and personal hygiene, autonomous drone systems, and next-generation coffee machines.40

  2. Strategy 2: Diversification of Operations and Supply Chain to Leverage Geographical Flexibility

    Description: In response to geopolitical uncertainties and shifting customer demand for production outside of China, Fu Yu is actively leveraging its manufacturing presence across Singapore, Malaysia, and China to offer geographical flexibility. This includes diversifying its customer and supplier base.41

    • Required Capabilities:
      • Existing manufacturing sites across Singapore, Malaysia, and China (6 strategic sites, 1.5 million sq ft capacity).42
      • Ability to manage and coordinate complex cross-border manufacturing operations.43
      • Established relationships with a diverse international customer base.44
      • Robust supply chain management framework with annual vendor evaluation assessments.45
      • Proactive global supplier diversification.46
    • Competitive and Historical Context:
      • "More customers seek to source production away from China."47
      • Fu Yu, as a Singapore-headquartered and SGX-listed company with production in three countries, can offer this flexibility.48
      • FY2024 saw Singapore and Malaysia operations gain revenue contribution (20.9 ppt and 24.9 ppt respectively), while China's contribution reduced by 7.1 ppt, reflecting this shift.49
      • Secured projects across broad spectrum of industries including medical devices and consumer segments, with higher sales in Singapore and Malaysia from these segments in FY2024.50
      • Ceased Supply Chain Management Services (FYSCS) segment to reallocate resources to core manufacturing, enhancing focus on key capabilities.51

  3. Strategy 3: Integration of Advanced Automation and Sustainable Technologies via Smart Factory

    Description: Fu Yu is actively integrating Industry 4.0 automation, 3D printing, and eco-friendly material usage within its flagship Smart Factory to enhance production efficiency, reduce waste, and align with sustainability goals. This includes transitioning to cleaner energy sources.52

    • Required Capabilities:
      • Flagship Smart Factory in Singapore capable of advanced precision manufacturing (e.g., 3D metal printing).53
      • Industry 4.0 machinery and optimized shopfloor layouts.54
      • Integrated cloud-based IT platform (ERP) for paperless operations.55
      • R&D and engineering capabilities to incorporate 3D printing and additive manufacturing.56
      • Collaboration capabilities with customers and suppliers for eco-friendly materials (e.g., bio-resins).57
      • Partnerships for renewable energy integration (solar panels).58
    • Competitive and Historical Context:
      • Smart Factory launched in FY2024, affirming its position as one of Asia's most advanced precision manufacturing facilities.59
      • Completed most of the testing for ERP integrated cloud-based IT platform by end FY2024.60
      • Installed solar panels on rooftops in Johor and Penang in addition to Singapore facilities, generating up to 3,233 MWh of renewable energy.61
      • "The Group is progressively replacing hydraulic machinery with electric alternatives to enhance operational efficiency and reduce oil consumption."62
      • Active discussions with suppliers and customers regarding the use of biodegradable bio-resin.63
      • Received Enterprise Singapore's Scale-Up SG Programme, sharpening focus on key segments.64

c. Disconnects between Strategic Objectives and Corporate Strategies:

While Fu Yu's objectives and strategies are largely aligned, a notable disconnect arises concerning the strategic ambition for 'sustainable growth and enhanced financial performance' in light of the recent challenges and eventual discontinuation of the Supply Chain Management Services (FYSCS) segment.

  • Operational and Governance Disconnect in FYSCS: The decision to discontinue the FYSCS segment was due to the "inability to identify a suitable candidate to effectively run and manage the business" and "significant weaknesses" highlighted by an internal audit, leading to an ongoing investigation into "purported misuse of FYSCS's resources" and "irregular expense claims."65 This situation reveals a significant disconnect:

    • Objective (Growth & Performance) vs. Strategy (Operational Oversight): The objective is to achieve sustainable growth and improve financial performance by emphasizing core manufacturing and strategic reallocation. However, the issues within FYSCS indicate a failure in initial due diligence, talent acquisition, operational oversight, and risk management processes for a segment that was initially considered a growth area. This undermines the foundational principles of controlled expansion and efficient resource utilization that are critical for achieving broader strategic financial objectives.
    • Implications: While the cessation of FYSCS allows for resource reallocation to the core manufacturing business (which is a sound strategic move in itself), the underlying reasons for its failure (management issues, governance weaknesses, potential financial improprieties) represent a historical operational and strategic misstep. The ongoing investigation and potential claims of S$1.67 million from Evertree Hongkong Limited indicate material financial and reputational risks that have materialized, directly impacting the financial performance in FY2024.66 The auditors have also issued a qualified opinion on the financial statements due to the ongoing investigation into FYSCS, indicating that its internal controls and risk management systems "may be inadequate."67 This suggests a past disconnect in ensuring adequate controls and governance in pursuit of business growth.
