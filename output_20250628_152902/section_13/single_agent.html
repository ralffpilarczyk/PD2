Section 13: Strategic Objectives and Corporate Strategies

This section extracts and analyzes Fu Yu Corporation Limited's strategic goals and corporate strategies based on the provided documents for the financial year ended 31 December 2024 (FY2024), along with more recent interim financial statements and presentations where applicable. Emphasis is placed on distinguishing between forward-looking objectives and present-day competitive strategies leveraging existing assets/capabilities.

a. Strategic Objectives

Strategic objectives are defined as goals going forward, noting timeframes, success metrics, and resource allocation.

  1. Enhance Core High-Precision Manufacturing Capabilities and Market Position
    • Objective: To strengthen and advance its position as a leading high-precision manufacturer, leveraging its Smart Factory and advanced capabilities to secure new, higher-margin projects and achieve long-term sustainable revenue.
      This includes stabilizing high-precision products at the 5-micron precision level and moving towards even higher levels of precision.
    • Timeframe/Horizon: Ongoing, with early successes noted in FY2024, contributing to future years' revenue. The move to higher precision levels is a continuous, forward-looking effort beyond FY2024.
    • Success Metrics/KPIs:
      • Higher order volumes and increased interest from existing and new customers. (FY2024: Order book increased 20% year-on-year, Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Translation of gestating high-precision projects into mass production and long-term sustainable revenue. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Achieving and surpassing the 5-micron precision level for products. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Increased revenue contribution from medical devices, drone technology, and smart appliances segments. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Improved financial performance with narrowed net loss and increased EBITDA from continuing operations. (FY2024: Net loss narrowed to S$3.9 million from S$10.1 million in FY2023; EBITDA increased to S$8.5 million from S$0.6 million. Fu Yu FS Q424, p. 30, Review of performance)
    • Resource Allocation:
      • Investment in advanced tooling with high precision. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4, Chairman's Message)
      • Introduction and utilization of a New Product Introduction ("NPI") team. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4, Chairman's Message)
      • Leveraging the flagship Smart Factory in Singapore, equipped with Industry 4.0 machinery and a 100K-class cleanroom. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Capital expenditure on factory equipment, plant, and machineries (FY2024: S$6.9 million). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 8, Financial Highlights; p. 9, Financial Position)
  2. Improve Operational Efficiency and Automation through Technology Adoption
    • Objective: To enhance production efficiency, reduce downtime, and manage costs through continuous improvement and the adoption of advanced technologies, especially Industry 4.0.
    • Timeframe/Horizon: From FY2024 onwards, with ERP integrated cloud-based IT platform fully operational. Automation efforts expected to increase efficiency for overnight and weekend production.
    • Success Metrics/KPIs:
      • Reduction of downtime by 40% compared to a typical facility through increased automation levels in the toolroom. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Completion of testing and implementation of Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) integrated cloud-based IT platform. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Decrease in energy consumption (FY2024: 2.5% decrease in purchased electricity compared to FY2023). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 38, Sustainability Report - Energy Consumption)
      • Reduction in Scope 2 overall GHG emissions by 30% by FY2030 (using FY2023 as baseline). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 40, Sustainability Report - Greenhouse Gas Emissions)
    • Resource Allocation:
      • Completion of testing for the ERP integrated cloud-based IT platform. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Investment in Industry 4.0 machinery and continuous replacement of hydraulic machinery with electric alternatives. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 32, Sustainability Report - TCFD; p. 46, Sustainability Report - Our Initiatives)
      • Installation of energy-efficient LED light fixtures. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 38, Sustainability Report - Energy Consumption)
      • Ongoing efforts in R&D and technology adoption for 3D printing and additive manufacturing. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 46, Sustainability Report - Our Initiatives)
  3. Strengthen Sustainability Practices and Responsible Business Operations
    • Objective: To embed sustainability into corporate strategy and core values, focusing on environmental stewardship, responsible consumption and production, and strong governance, ensuring long-term growth and stakeholder trust.
    • Timeframe/Horizon: Ongoing, with specific targets set for FY2030 (GHG emissions) and continuous monitoring of environmental and social metrics.
    • Success Metrics/KPIs:
      • Reduction in carbon emissions, specifically targeting a 30% reduction in Scope 2 overall emissions by FY2030 (from FY2023 baseline). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 40, Sustainability Report - Greenhouse Gas Emissions)
      • Increased generation of renewable energy (totaling up to 3,233 MWh from solar panels in Singapore, Johor, and Penang). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Maintaining or reducing water consumption at current levels. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 42, Sustainability Report - Water Consumption)
      • Achieving a zero-workplace incident rate and minimizing instances of employee ill health due to work-related causes. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 60, Sustainability Report - Occupational Health and Safety)
      • Maintaining zero confirmed incidents of corruption and non-compliance with environmental laws and regulations. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 63, Sustainability Report - Anti-corruption)
    • Resource Allocation:
      • Installation of solar panels on facility rooftops in Singapore, Johor, and Penang. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message; p. 46, Sustainability Report - Our Initiatives)
      • Investment in water-saving technologies and rainwater harvesting practices. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 41, Sustainability Report - Water Consumption)
      • Implementation of comprehensive waste management (3Rs approach: Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) and environmental compliance (RoHS, REACH). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 43, Sustainability Report - Waste; p. 46, Sustainability Report - Environmental-friendly Supply Chain)
      • Structured training and development programs for employees, including occupational health and safety (OHS) and anti-corruption policies. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 57, Sustainability Report - Training and Education; p. 63, Sustainability Report - Anti-corruption)

b. Corporate Strategies

Corporate strategies are defined as using existing assets or existing capabilities to compete now, ensuring feasibility, and listing required capabilities, competitive/historical context, and any disconnects.

  1. Strategic Focus on Core High-Precision Manufacturing
    • Strategy: The Group is intensely focusing on its core manufacturing business, particularly high-precision plastic and metal components, to drive growth and profitability. This involves leveraging advanced tooling, an NPI team, and its Smart Factory to secure high-value projects.
    • Required Capabilities:
      • Deep operating knowledge and expertise in high-end precision plastic and metal components manufacturing (45+ years of experience). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 3, Company Profile)
      • Advanced tooling capabilities, including complex mould inserts and high-precision 3D metal printing for rapid prototyping. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 3, Company Profile; p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Dedicated New Product Introduction (NPI) team for early-stage value addition in the business cycle. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4, Chairman's Message)
      • State-of-the-art Singapore Smart Factory, equipped with Industry 4.0 machinery and a 100K-class cleanroom for biomedical products. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 3, Company Profile; p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Vertically-integrated manufacturing services including product design, tooling design and fabrication, production, assembly, and testing. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 3, Company Profile)
    • Competitive and Historical Context:
      • This strategy is an "early success" of a transformation initiated in FY2023, resulting in a sharp improvement in the core manufacturing segment in FY2024 after more than a decade. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4, Chairman's Message)
      • Higher order volumes from existing customers and new project wins (biomedical, consumer, medical devices, drone technology, smart appliances) in FY2024 validate this focus. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4-5, Chairman's Message)
      • The Group is positioned as a trusted manufacturing partner for innovative, high-performance products, with projects involving high-precision tooling expected to translate into long-term sustainable revenue due to their "sticky" nature. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
  2. Geographical Diversification and Flexibility
    • Strategy: To offer geographical flexibility in manufacturing operations to mitigate geopolitical risks and meet evolving customer sourcing preferences, particularly customers seeking production alternatives to China.
    • Required Capabilities:
      • Operational manufacturing sites in Singapore, Malaysia, and China (6 strategic sites with over 1.5 million square feet production floor capacity). (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 3, Company Profile; p. 14, Our Network)
      • Existing customer base across diverse sectors (life sciences, medical, consumer, automotive, industrial) that can benefit from multi-country production capabilities. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 3, Company Profile)
      • Ability to manage and reallocate resources across different geographical locations. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • Existing supply chain resilience and global supplier network to support diversified production. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 34, Sustainability Report - TCFD)
    • Competitive and Historical Context:
      • Amid geopolitical uncertainty, more customers seek to source production away from China. Fu Yu's Singapore headquarters and presence in three countries allow it to offer this flexibility. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message)
      • This strategy is a response to supply chain disruptions experienced during the pandemic and ongoing geopolitical tensions. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4, Chairman's Message)
      • FY2024 saw a shift in revenue contribution by location, with Singapore and Malaysia operations gaining significant share while China's contribution reduced. (Singapore: +20.9 ppt to S$46.2 million; Malaysia: +24.9 ppt to S$36.6 million; China: -7.1 ppt to 27.9%. Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4, Chairman's Message)
  3. Value Chain Integration and Customer-Centric One-Stop Solutions
    • Strategy: To provide comprehensive, vertically integrated services that cover the entire product lifecycle from design to testing, enhancing value for customers and fostering long-term partnerships. This is achieved by offering a one-stop solution that simplifies the manufacturing process for clients.
    • Required Capabilities:
      • Product design, including New Production Introduction (NPI) and material selection services. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 18, Sustainability Report - Our Business)
      • Tooling design and fabrication, leveraging decades of tool-making expertise. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 18, Sustainability Report - Our Business)
      • Plastic injection moulding and metal injection moulding capabilities for intricate products and high-volume production. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 18, Sustainability Report - Our Business)
      • Box Build Assembly, offering comprehensive secondary processes and product assembly services. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 18, Sustainability Report - Our Business)
      • Customer relationship management, through regular meetings, satisfaction surveys, and after-sales follow-up. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 24, Sustainability Report - Stakeholder Engagement)
    • Competitive and Historical Context:
      • This strategy aims to "enhance its value add to customers and build mutually beneficial long-term partnerships." (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 3, Company Profile)
      • Historically, the Group has positioned itself as offering a "One-Stop Solution." (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 18, Sustainability Report - Our Business)
      • New project wins in biomedical and consumer segments in FY2024 were achieved through advanced tooling and the introduction of the NPI team, demonstrating the effectiveness of this integrated approach. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 4, Chairman's Message)

c. Disconnects between Strategic Objectives and Corporate Strategies

While Fu Yu Corporation Limited articulates clear strategic objectives and implements corporate strategies, one notable disconnect emerged during FY2024 relating to its now-discontinued supply chain management services segment.

  • Discontinued Supply Chain Management Services (Fu Yu Supply Chain Solutions Pte Ltd - FYSCS)
    • Objective (Implicit/Former Aspiration): To operate a successful and well-managed supply chain management services segment as part of the Group's diverse business portfolio, contributing to overall revenue and profitability.
    • Corporate Strategy (Former): To engage in providing supply chain management services for commodity raw materials, including sourcing, shipping, and supplying commodities within the Group and to external customers. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 18, Sustainability Report - Our Business)
    • Disconnect:
      • Despite being a significant contributor to overall revenue (39.9% of total revenue in FY2024, Fu Yu FS Q424, p. 29, Review of Performance), the Group ceased all business activities in FYSCS in the last quarter of FY2024. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message; Fu Yu FS Q424, p. 11, Note 1)
      • The cessation was explicitly due to the "inability to identify a suitable candidate to effectively run and manage the business" and "significant weaknesses in FYSCS's risk management process" highlighted by an internal audit report dated 26 July 2024. (Fu Yu FS Q424, p. 26, Note 15; Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message; p. 162, Corporate Governance Report - Principle 9)
      • Ongoing investigations into the affairs of FYSCS, including "unverifiable arrangements," "unauthorized usage of email accounts," and "irregular expense claims," leading to potential claims against individuals, indicate a severe breakdown in governance and operational control within this segment. (Fu Yu FS Q424, p. 26-27, Note 32; Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 101, Directors' Statement)
      • The external auditor issued a qualified opinion on the FY2024 financial statements due to the ongoing investigation into FYSCS, unable to determine the potential financial impact. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 104, Independent Auditor's Report)
    • Impact of Disconnect:
      • This represents a significant disconnect where a business segment, while contributing to topline revenue, failed to meet operational, governance, and management expectations, leading to its complete discontinuation and resource reallocation. This undermines the implicit strategic objective of maintaining a diversified and profitably managed portfolio segment.
      • Resources previously allocated to FYSCS are now being reallocated to the core manufacturing business to sharpen focus. (Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, Chairman's Message; Fu Yu FS Q424, p. 30, Commentary)
