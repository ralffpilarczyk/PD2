## SECTION 21: Sellside Positioning - Competitive Positioning

Fu Yu Corporation Limited ("Fu Yu" or the "Company") is Singapore's oldest and one of Asia's largest manufacturers of high-end precision plastic and metal components and products. As of FY2024, the Group has strategically pivoted to focus solely on its core manufacturing business, having ceased all activities in its supply chain management services segment in the last quarter of FY2024. This analysis focuses on the competitive advantages of its continuing manufacturing operations.

While Fu Yu's manufacturing segment demonstrated a positive trajectory with revenue growth and margin improvement in FY2024, the Group as a whole remained in a net loss position (S\$3.9 million in FY2024, narrowed from S\$10.1 million in FY2023) due to the discontinued supply chain segment's losses and impairments. The ongoing investigation into Fu Yu Supply Chain Solutions Pte Ltd ("FYSCS") has resulted in a qualified audit opinion for FY2024 (AR 24, p.104), highlighting weaknesses in internal controls and potential financial claims. This presents a significant non-operational risk that could impact overall investor perception and financial flexibility, despite management's assertion that it does not impact continuing operations (AR 24, p.6). This qualified opinion underscores a critical governance concern that investors must weigh against the operational improvements in the core business.

FuFu Yu's manufacturing segment demonstrated a 10.6% revenue growth in FY2024, reaching S\$114.9 million, driven by higher order volumes and new projects in the biomedical and consumer segments (AR 24, p.6, p.8). This growth, coupled with a 28.1% increase in gross profit to S\$15.5 million, led to a significant 1.8 percentage point improvement in gross margin, from 11.7% in FY2023 to 13.5% in FY2024 (AR 24, p.8). Critically, the manufacturing segment reversed its S\$7.3 million operating loss in FY2023 to a S\$0.4 million operating profit in FY2024, indicating a substantial turnaround in core operational efficiency (AR 24, p.8). This improvement is further supported by a 20% year-on-year increase in the order book at the end of FY2024, signaling continued positive momentum (AR 24, p.6).

### Competitive Advantages

Fu Yu's competitive positioning in the high-end precision plastic and metal components manufacturing sector is underpinned by three key advantages: deep technical expertise and high-precision capabilities, long-standing and diversified customer relationships, and a strategic geographic footprint coupled with operational excellence.

**1. Deep Technical Expertise & High-Precision Capabilities:**
Fu Yu differentiates itself through its ability to manufacture complex, high-tolerance components for demanding industries such as life sciences, medical, consumer, automotive, and industrial sectors (AR 24, p.2). This capability is rooted in its "flagship Smart Factory" in Singapore, described as "one of Asia's most advanced precision manufacturing facilities" (AR 24, p.2, p.6). The Smart Factory is equipped for "complex mould inserts, high precision tooling, and 3D metal printing" and features a "100K-class cleanroom for manufacturing and assembling biomedical products" (AR 24, p.2, p.6). The Company's New Product Introduction ("NPI") team further enhances its value proposition by collaborating with customers in early stages of product development, enabling "advanced tooling with high precision" (AR 24, p.6). Management also highlights efforts to stabilize "high-precision products at the 5-micron precision level and moving up the value chain to produce even higher levels of precision" (AR 24, p.7). This technical prowess allows Fu Yu to target higher-margin product categories and secure new projects, as evidenced by new wins in medical devices, drone technology, and smart appliances in FY2024 (AR 24, p.6). The ability to produce such specialized components typically commands pricing premiums and reduces price sensitivity compared to generalist manufacturers, contributing directly to the observed gross margin improvement from 11.7% to 13.5% in the manufacturing segment (AR 24, p.8).

**2. Long-Standing, Diversified Customer Relationships & One-Stop Solution:**
With over 45 years of operating knowledge, Fu Yu has cultivated an "international, diverse and loyal customer base" (AR 24, p.2). The Company emphasizes providing a "one-stop solution" including product design, tooling design and fabrication, production, assembly, and testing, which fosters "mutually beneficial long-term partnerships" (AR 24, p.2). This integrated service offering creates significant customer stickiness, particularly for projects involving high-precision tooling, which management expects to "translate into long-term sustainable revenue" (AR 24, p.7). The refreshed customer base now includes "global leaders in wound care and personal hygiene, autonomous drone systems, and next-generation coffee machines" (AR 24, p.6). This diversification across industries and geographies helps mitigate client concentration risk and provides a more stable revenue base. The 20% year-on-year increase in the order book at the end of FY2024 (AR 24, p.6) further supports the strength of these relationships and their translation into future revenue stability and growth, potentially leading to lower customer acquisition costs compared to competitors.

**3. Strategic Geographic Footprint & Operational Excellence:**
Fu Yu operates "6 strategic manufacturing sites across Singapore, Malaysia, and China," totaling "more than 1.5 million square feet of production floor capacity" (AR 24, p.2). This multi-country presence offers geographical flexibility, which is increasingly valuable "amid geopolitical uncertainty" and a trend of customers seeking to "source production away from China" (AR 24, p.6). The shift in revenue contribution, with Singapore and Malaysia operations gaining 20.9 percentage points and 24.9 percentage points respectively in FY2024, while China's contribution reduced by 7.1 percentage points (AR 24, p.6), demonstrates the adaptability and strategic advantage of this diversified footprint. Operational excellence is further pursued through investments in automation, such as the ERP integrated cloud-based IT platform and plans to increase automation levels in the toolroom to reduce downtime by 40% (AR 24, p.7). The installation of solar panels across facilities in Singapore, Johor, and Penang, generating up to 3,233 MWh of renewable energy (AR 24, p.6), also contributes to cost efficiencies and a "greener, more responsible footprint" (AR 24, p.2). These efficiencies contribute to improved operating margins, as seen in the manufacturing segment's shift from a 7.0% operating loss to a 0.3% operating profit in FY2024.

### Comparative Analysis and Financial Prospects

While direct comparative financial data for named competitors like Sunningdale Tech or Venture Corporation is not available in the provided source documents, Fu Yu's internal financial improvements in its manufacturing segment suggest a strengthening competitive position. The 1.8 percentage point improvement in gross margin and the significant swing from operating loss to profit in the manufacturing segment (AR 24, p.8) indicate enhanced pricing power and/or cost management relative to its own past performance. The ability to secure new projects with "global leaders" (AR 24, p.6) and grow its order book by 20% (AR 24, p.6) in a "challenging operating environment" (AR 24, p.6) suggests that its technical capabilities and customer relationships are resonating in the market.

The strategic pivot away from the loss-making supply chain segment, despite the associated investigation and qualified audit opinion, is intended to allow Fu Yu to reallocate resources and sharpen its focus on its core, higher-margin manufacturing business (AR 24, p.6). This strategic clarity, combined with ongoing investments in advanced manufacturing (Smart Factory, 5-micron precision, automation) and a diversified geographic presence, positions Fu Yu to potentially achieve superior financial prospects relative to generalist manufacturers. The "sticky" nature of high-precision tooling projects (AR 24, p.7) implies more predictable and sustainable revenue streams, which can lead to higher valuation multiples compared to businesses with less stable demand. The company's healthy net cash position of S\$55.5 million (AR 24, p.8) provides financial flexibility for continued investment in these competitive advantages.

However, the ongoing investigation into FYSCS and the resulting qualified audit opinion (AR 24, p.104) remain a material non-operational risk. While management asserts it does not impact continuing operations, such issues can negatively affect investor perception, access to capital, and overall financial flexibility, potentially offsetting some of the positive operational momentum. The recent arbitration ruling against FYSCS for S\$1.67 million (AR 24, p.171) further highlights the financial liabilities stemming from the discontinued segment, which could strain the Group's overall financial health and divert management attention. Investors will closely monitor the resolution of these claims and the broader investigation.
