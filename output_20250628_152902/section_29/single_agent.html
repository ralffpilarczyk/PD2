Section 29: Buyside Due Diligence - Operating Performance

Overall, Fu Yu Corporation Limited's operating performance over the last 24 months demonstrates a pivotal strategic shift and a strengthening of its core manufacturing capabilities. Despite a challenging global environment and the eventual discontinuation of its lower-margin supply chain management services segment, the Company has successfully driven robust growth in its manufacturing revenue, significantly expanded its gross profit margins through a focus on high-precision products and cost optimization, and made substantial capital investments to enhance future capacity and efficiency. This performance indicates a resilient and forward-looking operational strategy, positioning the Company for sustainable long-term value creation in its core business.

1. Manufacturing Segment Revenue Growth

  • Definition and Relevance: Manufacturing Segment Revenue represents the total sales generated from the Company's core business of manufacturing and sub-assembly of precision plastic parts and components, and fabrication of precision moulds and dies. It is a critical operating metric as it directly reflects the demand for the Company's high-value engineering services, its ability to secure and fulfill orders, and its market traction in key industries (life sciences, medical, consumer, automotive, industrial). Consistent growth in this segment indicates increasing market share and/or expansion into new, higher-value product categories, directly impacting the Company's top-line economic performance.
  • Historical Data for the Last 24 Months:
    • FY2023: S$103.9 million1
    • FY2024: S$114.9 million2
    • 1H2023: S$51.3 million3
    • 2H2023: S$52.6 million (calculated: FY2023 S$103.9M - 1H2023 S$51.3M)
    • 1H2024: S$60.1 million4
    • 2H2024: S$54.8 million (calculated: FY2024 S$114.9M - 1H2024 S$60.1M)

    Trend: The manufacturing segment revenue has shown a steady upward trend over the last 24 months, with an overall 10.5% growth from FY2023 to FY20245. Quarterly data reveals strong performance in 1H2024 (17.1% YoY growth compared to 1H2023), followed by a slight sequential decline in 2H2024, although still demonstrating growth compared to 2H2023 (4.2% YoY growth).

  • Analysis of Drivers: The growth in manufacturing revenue is primarily driven by:
    • Increased Order Volumes: The Company secured higher order volumes from existing customers, indicating strong customer relationships and satisfaction. The order book at the end of FY2024 increased 20% year-on-year6.
    • New Project Wins: Significant project wins in the biomedical and consumer segments, particularly driven by the introduction of the New Product Introduction ("NPI") team and advanced tooling capabilities, contributed to the topline growth. New projects include microfluidic chips for an international biomedical company and high-precision tools for drug delivery devices7.
    • Geographical Shift: Higher sales contributions from Singapore (+20.9% to S$46.2M in FY2024) and Malaysia (+24.9% to S$36.6M in FY2024) offset a decline in China operations (-11.8% to S$32.1M in FY2024)8. This shift is also supported by the geopolitical trend of customers sourcing production away from China9.
  • Sustainability of Current Performance Trend: The Company's manufacturing revenue trend appears sustainable, underpinned by its strategic transformation. The increase in order book and the "sticky" nature of high-precision projects ready for mass production are expected to translate into long-term sustainable revenue10. Continued investment in the Smart Factory, advanced tooling, and new capabilities (e.g., 5-micron precision, 3D metal printing, 100K-class cleanroom) positions the Company to capture higher-margin opportunities in medical technology and biotechnology sectors11,12. Factors that could cause deterioration include prolonged global economic slowdown, intensified competition, or unforeseen supply chain disruptions.
  • Quantified Financial Impact: The 10.5% growth in manufacturing revenue from S$103.9 million in FY2023 to S$114.9 million in FY2024 directly contributed S$11.0 million to the Group's overall revenue. This growth in the core segment was crucial in offsetting the S$10.0 million decline in the now-discontinued supply chain management services segment (FY2023 S$86.4M to FY2024 S$76.4M)13, thereby preserving the Group's modest 0.5% overall topline increase.
  • Due Diligence Questions:
    1. What is the current breakdown of manufacturing revenue by end-market (e.g., life sciences, medical, consumer, automotive, industrial) and how does the Company plan to further diversify or concentrate efforts in specific higher-growth segments given its strategic shift towards high-precision products?
    2. Can the Company provide details on the specific new projects secured in 1H2024 (e.g., contract value, expected duration, and key milestones) and what is the current utilization rate of the Smart Factory and other key manufacturing assets to support the projected 20% order book increase?

2. Manufacturing Segment Gross Profit Margin (GPM)

  • Definition and Relevance: Manufacturing Segment Gross Profit Margin (GPM) is calculated as manufacturing gross profit divided by manufacturing revenue, expressed as a percentage. It measures the profitability of the Company's core manufacturing operations after accounting for the cost of goods sold. This metric is highly relevant as it indicates the Company's pricing power, efficiency in production, and ability to control direct manufacturing costs (labor, raw materials, overheads). Improvements in GPM directly translate to enhanced profitability and cash generation from core operations.
  • Historical Data for the Last 24 Months:
    • FY2023: 11.7%14
    • FY2024: 13.5%15
    • 1H2023: 11.56% (calculated: Gross Profit S$5.934M / Revenue S$51.323M)16
    • 2H2023: 11.86% (calculated: (FY2023 Gross Profit S$12.169M - 1H2023 Gross Profit S$5.934M) / (FY2023 Revenue S$103.941M - 1H2023 Revenue S$51.323M))17,18
    • 1H2024: 14.09% (calculated: Gross Profit S$8.471M / Revenue S$60.138M)19
    • 2H2024: 12.87% (calculated: (FY2024 Gross Profit S$15.516M - 1H2024 Gross Profit S$8.471M) / (FY2024 Revenue S$114.888M - 1H2024 Revenue S$60.138M))20,21

    Trend: The manufacturing segment GPM has shown a notable improvement over the last 24 months, increasing from 11.7% in FY2023 to 13.5% in FY202422. Quarterly analysis shows a strong uplift in 1H2024 to 14.09% from 11.56% in 1H2023, followed by a slight moderation to 12.87% in 2H2024. This indicates sustained operational efficiency gains.

  • Analysis of Drivers: The improvement in manufacturing GPM is primarily driven by:
    • Strategic Transformation & High-Precision Focus: The Company's strategic shift towards high-precision manufacturing and advanced tooling, along with new projects in biomedical and consumer segments, allows it to command higher unit pricing and capture higher-margin product categories24.
    • New Product Introduction (NPI) Team: The NPI team's ability to add value in the early stages of the business cycle has instilled confidence in new customers, particularly for higher-margin products, facilitating profitable project wins25.
    • Cost Optimization: Lower selling and administrative expenses, attributed to reduced employee costs, travelling, and utility charges, indirectly contribute to overall profitability, supporting margin expansion26. Investments in energy-efficient electric machines to replace older hydraulic machines, and upgrades to LED lighting also contribute to cost reduction27.
    • Cessation of Low-Margin Segment: The discontinuation of the low-margin supply chain management services segment (which had a gross profit margin of 1.9% in FY2024 compared to manufacturing's 13.5%) improved the overall Group GPM, allowing the manufacturing segment's strong performance to shine through more clearly28.
  • Sustainability of Current Performance Trend: The positive trend in manufacturing GPM appears sustainable, underpinned by the Company's ongoing strategic transformation to higher-value-added services and products. Continued focus on advanced tooling, automation (e.g., ERP integration, increased automation in toolroom), and R&D into higher precision levels (e.g., 5-micron precision, moving to even higher levels) should further enhance unit margins and reduce costs29. Factors that could challenge this sustainability include intense pricing pressure in competitive markets, unexpected increases in raw material or energy costs, or a failure to continuously innovate and secure high-value projects.
  • Quantified Financial Impact: The 1.8 percentage point increase in manufacturing GPM (from 11.7% in FY2023 to 13.5% in FY2024) directly contributed an additional S$3.3 million in gross profit (S$15.5M in FY2024 vs S$12.2M in FY2023)30. This margin expansion was a key factor in the sharp narrowing of the net loss from continuing operations from S$7.6 million in FY2023 to S$1.2 million in FY202431.
  • Due Diligence Questions:
    1. What specific initiatives are underway to further enhance manufacturing efficiency and maintain or expand GPM, especially considering the slight sequential decline in 2H2024 GPM, and what are the targets for GPM improvement in FY2025 and beyond?
    2. How is the Company managing the cost of raw materials, particularly in light of global supply chain volatility, to mitigate potential downward pressure on gross margins for its high-precision products?

3. Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) on Property, Plant & Equipment (PPE)

  • Definition and Relevance: Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) on Property, Plant & Equipment (PPE) represents the funds spent by the Company to acquire, upgrade, and maintain its physical assets such as factory equipment, plant, and machinery. This is a crucial operating metric because it reflects the Company's commitment to enhancing its production capabilities, improving operational efficiency, investing in advanced technologies (e.g., Smart Factory, automation), and expanding capacity. While a cash outflow, it underpins future revenue generation, efficiency gains, and competitive positioning, indirectly impacting asset utilization and unit economics in the long term.
  • Historical Data for the Last 24 Months:
    • FY2023: S$3.96 million32
    • FY2024: S$6.94 million33
    • 1H2023: S$1.84 million34
    • 2H2023: S$2.12 million (calculated: FY2023 S$3.96M - 1H2023 S$1.84M)
    • 1H2024: S$3.07 million35
    • 2H2024: S$3.87 million (calculated: FY2024 S$6.94M - 1H2024 S$3.07M)

    Trend: The Company's CAPEX on PPE has significantly increased over the last 24 months, almost doubling from S$3.96 million in FY2023 to S$6.94 million in FY2024. Quarterly data shows a consistent ramp-up in investment, with 2H2024 CAPEX being the highest in the observed period.

  • Benchmarking: The provided documents do not offer specific competitor or industry average data for CAPEX. Therefore, outperformance or underperformance against industry benchmarks cannot be quantified. However, the Company states its CAPEX is "mainly for the purchase of factory equipment, plant and machineries" and mentions its "flagship Smart Factory in Singapore is one of Asia's most advanced precision manufacturing facilities," implying a commitment to staying competitive through investment36,37.
  • Analysis of Drivers: The substantial increase in CAPEX is driven by:
    • Strategic Transformation & Technology Upgrades: The Company is investing in advanced manufacturing capabilities, such as Industry 4.0 machinery and replacing older hydraulic machines with energy-efficient electric alternatives, as part of its Smart Factory initiatives in Singapore. This enables complex mould inserts, high-precision tooling, and 3D metal printing38,39,40.
    • Capacity Expansion for New Projects: The CAPEX supports the scaling up of production for new projects secured in higher-margin categories like biomedical and consumer segments, and future projects for electric vehicles (EVs) and medical devices41,42.
    • Efficiency and Automation Initiatives: Investments aim to increase automation levels in the toolroom to reduce downtime by 40% and improve overall plant performance. Solar panel installations also contribute, aimed at reducing energy costs and carbon footprint43,44.
  • Sustainability of Current Performance Trend: The elevated CAPEX trend is sustainable given the Company's healthy balance sheet with net cash of S$55.5 million as at 31 December 202445. This investment is a long-term strategic commitment to maintaining technological leadership and capturing future growth opportunities in high-value precision manufacturing. Factors that could affect this trend include changes in market demand that do not justify the investment, or a significant shift in the Company's strategic priorities away from capital-intensive technological upgrades.
  • Quantified Financial Impact: The increased CAPEX of S$6.94 million in FY2024 (a S$2.98 million increase from FY2023) represents a significant cash outflow from investing activities46. While a cost in the short term, this investment is expected to contribute to future revenue growth by enabling new project wins and capacity expansion, and to margin expansion by improving operational efficiency and reducing energy costs, thereby enhancing long-term economic performance.
  • Due Diligence Questions:
    1. What is the expected ROI and payback period for the recent CAPEX investments, particularly those related to the Smart Factory automation and new machinery, and how are these investments tracked against their intended efficiency and capacity goals?
    2. Given the strategic focus on high-precision products, what percentage of future CAPEX is allocated to advanced tooling and biomedical cleanroom capabilities, and how does this align with the long-term revenue targets for these segments?
