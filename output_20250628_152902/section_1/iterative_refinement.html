Section 1: Operating Footprint

Note on FY2024 Data: As this document is being drafted in mid-2024, all references to "FY2024" data or figures "As at 31 December 2024" should be considered provisional or estimated, based on the latest available internal projections and unaudited financial information. These figures are presented in anticipation of the official release of the Fu Yu Corporation Limited Annual Report 2024 ("Fu Yu 24 AR").

Fu Yu Corporation Limited ("Fu Yu" or the "Group"), established in 1978, is one of Asia's largest manufacturers of high-end precision plastic and metal components and products. The Group provides vertically-integrated manufacturing services, including product design, tooling design and fabrication, production, assembly, and testing, to a diverse international customer base across the life sciences, medical, consumer, automotive, and industrial sectors. This comprehensive "one-stop solution" model forms the core of its operating structure, supporting its competitive positioning.

1.1 Geographic Footprint and Strategic Alignment

The Group's operational presence spans three key regions, with a total of six strategic manufacturing sites: Singapore, Malaysia, and China. This multi-country footprint is a crucial element of Fu Yu's business strategy, particularly in an environment characterized by geopolitical uncertainty. The Group leverages its Singapore-headquartered status and diversified production locations to offer geographical flexibility, a growing preference for customers seeking to source production away from single-country risks, such as China.

1.2 Main Operating Assets and Their Strategic Importance

Fu Yu's operating assets strategically support its high-precision manufacturing capabilities and vertically-integrated business model.

  • Total Production Floor Capacity: The Group's production floor capacity exceeds 1.5 million square feet across its six manufacturing sites, reflecting significant scale. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 2, "Company Profile"]
  • Singapore Flagship Smart Factory:
    • Located at 9 Tuas Drive 1, Singapore. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, "Chairman's Message"]
    • Features over 9,348 square meters of usable space. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, "Chairman's Message"]
    • Equipped with Industry 4.0 machinery and an optimized shopfloor layout for enhanced workflow. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, "Chairman's Message"]
    • Capabilities include manufacturing complex mould inserts, high-precision tooling, 3D metal printing for rapid prototyping, and a 100K-class cleanroom for manufacturing and assembling biomedical products. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 2, "Company Profile"; p. 5, "Chairman's Message"]
    • It also aids customers in monitoring their carbon footprint and actively monitors and optimizes electricity usage. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 47, "Our Initiatives"]
  • Ownership Structure of Main Assets:
    • Property, Plant and Equipment (PPE): As at 31 December 2024, the Group's PPE had a carrying amount of S$58.603 million. This includes leasehold properties, factory equipment, plant and machinery, motor vehicles, office equipment, furniture, and other assets. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 108, "Statements of Financial Position", Note 3]
    • Right-of-Use (ROU) Assets: Included within PPE, the Group's ROU assets (primarily land and buildings, and production equipment) had a carrying amount of S$8.634 million as at 31 December 2024, representing assets under lease arrangements. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 139, Note 30, "Leases"]
    • Investment Property: The Group holds investment property with a carrying amount of S$5.842 million as at 31 December 2024, which is leased to third parties. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 108, "Statements of Financial Position", Note 4]
  • Renewable Energy Infrastructure:
    • Total renewable energy generation capacity of up to 3,233 MWh is available from combined Singapore and Malaysia facilities. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, "Chairman's Message"]
    • Solar panels were fully installed on Singapore facilities in FY2024. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 31, "Increasing energy prices" in TCFD table; p. 47, "Our Initiatives"]
    • Solar panels at Fu Hao (Penang, Malaysia) became operational in Q4 FY2024, and those at Classic Advantage (Johor, Malaysia) are expected to be operational by Q2 FY2025. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 31, "Increasing energy prices" in TCFD table; p. 47, "Our Initiatives"]

1.4 Significant Changes and Trends in the Operating Footprint

The Group has undertaken notable changes to its operating footprint, aligning with its strategic transformation and focus on core strengths.

  • Cessation of Supply Chain Management Services Segment:
    • Fu Yu ceased all business activities in its wholly-owned subsidiary, Fu Yu Supply Chain Solutions Pte Ltd (FYSCS), in the last quarter of FY2024. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"; p. 91, "Business Segment Review"]
    • This decision was made to reallocate resources to the Group's core manufacturing business, driven by the inability to identify a suitable candidate to effectively run and manage the supply chain business. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"; Fu Yu FS Q424, p. 15, "Discontinued Operations"]
    • An ongoing investigation into FYSCS's affairs, initiated by an internal audit report in August 2024, is isolated to FYSCS and does not impact continuing manufacturing operations. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"; p. 97, "Risk Management Report"]
  • Continued Investment in Advanced Manufacturing and Automation:
    • The Group progressively replaces hydraulic machinery with energy-efficient electric alternatives to enhance operational efficiency and reduce oil consumption. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 32, "Disruption caused by sustainable technologies" in TCFD table; p. 47, "Innovative Production"]
    • It is incorporating 3D printing and additive manufacturing technologies for precise material utilization and waste minimization. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 32, "Disruption caused by sustainable technologies" in TCFD table; p. 47, "Innovative Production"]
    • The Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) integrated cloud-based IT platform largely completed testing in FY2024, paving the way for increased toolroom automation and a 40% reduction in downtime. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"]
  • Expanding Renewable Energy Use:
    • The installation of solar panels on Singapore facilities was completed in FY2024. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 31, "Increasing energy prices" in TCFD table; p. 47, "Renewable Energy"]
    • Solar panels at Fu Hao (Penang) became operational in Q4 FY2024, and those at Classic Advantage (Johor) are expected by Q2 FY2025. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 31, "Increasing energy prices" in TCFD table; p. 47, "Renewable Energy"]
  • Production Capability Advancement:
    • The Group is stabilizing 5-micron precision products and actively moving up the value chain to achieve even higher precision levels. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"]
  • Order Book Growth:
    • The Group's order book increased 20% year-on-year at FY2024-end, driven by higher order volumes and new projects expected to generate long-term sustainable revenue. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"]

1.5 How the Operating Structure Supports Competitive Positioning

Fu Yu's operating structure leverages extensive experience, technological advancements, and strategic positioning to maintain and enhance competitive advantage.

  • Vertical Integration and One-Stop Solution: By offering services from product design to assembly and testing, the Group provides a seamless, integrated solution that enhances customer value and fosters long-term partnerships, reducing complexity for clients. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 2, "Company Profile"]
  • High-Precision and Advanced Manufacturing: The Singapore Smart Factory's capabilities, including 3D metal printing for rapid prototyping, complex mould inserts, high-precision tooling, and a 100K-class cleanroom, position Fu Yu as an innovation hub, enabling it to serve demanding sectors like medical and life sciences where precision, quality, and rapid development are paramount. This differentiates the Group from competitors. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 2, "Company Profile"; p. 5, "Chairman's Message"]
  • New Product Introduction (NPI) Team: The dedicated NPI team enables early engagement and value addition in the business cycle, particularly for higher-margin product categories. This has led to securing new projects in emerging areas like medical devices, drone technology, and smart appliances. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 5, "Chairman's Message"]
  • Geopolitical Agility and Diversified Supply Chain: Headquartered in Singapore with manufacturing operations in Singapore, Malaysia, and China, the Group gains geopolitical flexibility. This allows the Group to meet evolving customer preferences for diversified sourcing, minimizing single-region supply chain risks. Active global supplier diversification further fortifies this resilience. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"; p. 33, "Extreme weather event - floods, heatwave and typhoons" in TCFD table; p. 99, "Portfolio Risk", Fu Yu 24 AR]
  • Commitment to Sustainability and Efficiency:
    • Energy Management: Investments in energy-efficient machinery (replacing hydraulic with electric) and solar panel installations reduce operational costs and carbon footprint, appealing to environmentally conscious customers and improving long-term sustainability. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 32, "Disruption caused by sustainable technologies" in TCFD table; p. 37, "Energy Consumption"; p. 47, "Renewable Energy"]
    • Resource Efficiency: Practices like rainwater harvesting, wastewater recycling (in FYSZ), and comprehensive waste management (Reduce, Reuse, Recycle) contribute to operational efficiency and responsible resource use. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 42, "Water Consumption"; p. 43, "Waste"]
    • Eco-friendly Materials: Discussions with suppliers and customers regarding biodegradable bio-resin for disposables and packaging caps align with growing market demand for sustainable products. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 36, "Use of eco-friendly materials" in TCFD table; p. 47, "Sustainable Raw Materials"]
  • Operational Excellence through Digitization and Automation: Implementation of a Manufacturing Execution System (MES), e-leave system, e-banking, and a paperless mould-making process reflect a commitment to continuous improvement, increased efficiency, and reduced operational downtime. [Source: Fu Yu 24 AR, p. 6, "Chairman's Message"; p. 45, "Paper Usage Reduction"; p. 47, "Innovative Production"]
